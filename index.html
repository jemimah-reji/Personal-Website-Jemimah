Projects

Kids-U Staff Website - EPICS Project

As part of the EPICS (Engineering Projects in Community Service) program at the University of Texas at Dallas, our team collaborated with Kids-U, a nonprofit organization dedicated to supporting underprivileged youth, to enhance their staff website. The goal was to improve backend functionality and optimize the system for better staff management and operational efficiency.

  • Requirement Gathering and Analysis: Conducted detailed analysis sessions with Kids-U staff to identify existing pain points and gather requirements for the enhanced website.
  • Backend Development: Developed new backend features using C# in Visual Studio, including enhanced data management capabilities, automated form handling, and backend logic to streamline staff workflows.
  • User Interface Enhancements: Implemented visual changes and structural updates to the web interface, making the site more user-friendly and easier for staff to navigate critical functionalities.
  • Testing and Quality Assurance: Performed rigorous testing, including functionality checks, usability testing, and debugging to ensure the website met the nonprofit’s operational needs.
  • Team Collaboration: Worked closely with a team, applying project management skills to coordinate tasks, meet deadlines, and adapt to feedback from Kids-U stakeholders.

The EPICS program at UT Dallas emphasizes teamwork, problem-solving, and professional skills by engaging students in real-world community challenges. This project provided valuable hands-on experience, allowing us to directly impact the community by leveraging our technical skills in service of a nonprofit’s mission.
